Gyros

Description

A gyro is a cooked meat dish, typically lamb or chicken, wrapped in pita bread, almost like a sandwich. The wrap can include other ingredients such as tomatoes, onion, lettuce, and tzatziki sauce. The meat is typically cooked on a vertical rotisserie and served hot.

The gyro trace its origins to Greece. Cutting slices off of stacked meat was first seen in Bursa, part of the Ottoman Empire, called the doner kebab. It was introduced in Athens by immigrants from Anatolia and the Middle East. The distinct Greek dish later become known as the gyro including the definitive tzatziki.

Ingredients

  • pita wrap
  • rotisserie sliced meat: chicken, pork, or lamb
  • tzatziki sauce
  • diced tomatoes
  • chopped lettuce

Steps

  1. cook rotisserie meat, cutting off slices
  2. heat pita, spreading tzatziki over the wrap
  3. put meat, tomatoes, and chopped lettuce on wrap
  4. add a final dollop of tzatziki on top
